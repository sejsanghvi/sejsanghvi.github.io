UX / UI Design for TNS Course History Platform

A collaborative project to enhance the course registration process at The New School (TNS) with the introduction of a Course History Platform. This project aims to streamline and improve access to comprehensive course information for students, offering detailed insights into courses, faculty, and past student experiences, all in one convenient platform.

Main Goal

The main goal is to optimize the course selection experience at TNS through a user-centric Course History Platform, enhancing accessibility to detailed course information and enriching student decision-making.

My Role

UX Research, Wireframe and Prototyping, Visual Design, High-Fidelity Designs, Usability Testing, Accessibility, Iterative Improvement, Design Handoff

Tools

Figma, Fig Jam, Google Forms, Google Sheets

Background

The current class registration process at The New School presents a significant challenge as it lacks comprehensive information about classes making it difficult to make informed decisions about the course selections.

Designer's Note

As an international student at The New School, navigating scattered sources and incomplete details to find crucial course information before registration has been challenging. This project is deeply personal, as it aims to centralize comprehensive course details on one platform, with a focus on improving usability and clarity.



Let's Dig Deeper into the Specifics

Problem Statement

The current class registration process at The New School presents a significant challenge as it lacks comprehensive information about classes making it difficult to make informed decisions about the course selections.

Proposed Solution

Focus on providing deep insights into class content, expectations, and the academic atmosphere and facilitate connections with past students for valuable insights, ensuring a more informed and empowering academic journey to enrich the course selection process.

Competitive Analysis

NYU Rate My Courses

Rate My Professor


Subjectivity and Anonymity:

Platforms like "Rate My Professor" struggle with subjectivity due to user anonymity, allowing biased and unfair evaluations. Lack of accountability leads to inconsistent, unverified feedback, undermining student trust in information accuracy.

Incomplete Professor Evaluation:

Existing platforms often lack a comprehensive view of a professor's teaching abilities due to a lack of standardized evaluation processes. This inconsistency makes it challenging for students to form a balanced understanding of a professor's performance.

Insufficient Course Information:

Platforms such as "Rate My Courses" at NYU may lack specific course details, hindering students from understanding classes fully. Limited insights into teaching styles and overall academic experiences leave students without crucial information for informed course selections.

Need for a Tailored and Reliable Solution:

Addressing these challenges requires a robust platform offering accurate, detailed insights into professors and courses. Prioritizing transparency, accountability, and standardized evaluation methods, this solution aims to empower students with reliable information for informed academic decisions.

Key Takeaways

Streamlining Course Information Access

Students struggle to access clear information on class expectations, learning outcomes, and teaching styles before registration, often needing to navigate dispersed sources like the Course Catalog and unofficial channels.

Boosting Student Engagement with Reviews

Introducing a review system within courses could increase student participation, fostering valuable feedback sharing and improving overall course understanding for the academic community.

Immediate Feedback Limitations in Course Evaluation

The impact of course evaluations on student experience is hindered by the lack of actionable feedback, compounded by concerns over biased or negative reviews.

Leveraging Feedback for Course Improvement

A feedback system that offers actionable insights can enhance future classes by addressing current student needs and mitigating potential issues from public reviews.
