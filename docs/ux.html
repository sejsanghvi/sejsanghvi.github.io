UX Design for PRECISIONeffect's Accessibility Training Guide

Designed a guide aiming to empower designers at PRECISIONeffect to create more accessible user interfaces, particularly for users with disabilities. Through thorough research on web accessibility, a pseudo website with relevant Dos and Don’ts pop-ups were created to accompany the training guide.

Main Goal

To equip designers at PRECISIONeffect with the knowledge and tools to enhance the accessibility of their user interfaces, particularly for users with disabilities.

My Role

UX Research, Accessibility Research, Visual Design, Branding, Iterative Improvement, UI Design, Responsive Design, Design Handoff

Tools

XD, Adobe Illustrator

Background

Background: Precisioneffect, a leading pharma-based advertising agency, serves a diverse audience that includes a significant number of individuals with disabilities. This project aims to empower their design team with enhanced skills in creating accessible user interfaces tailored to meet these specific needs.

Designer's Note

As an intern at Precisioneffect, I conducted extensive user research and studied WCAG and accessibility guidelines. My role was to translate this research into practical graphics and websites. These resources were designed to educate our team on the critical aspects of accessibility in design, promoting inclusive and user-friendly digital solutions.

What is Accessible Design?

Accessible design involves eliminating barriers in digital content to ensure equal access for individuals with disabilities. It enhances overall user experience and satisfaction, proving most effective when integrated from the project’s outset. This proactive approach creates a more inclusive online environment, akin to providing a ramp for digital accessibility, ensuring everyone can navigate the virtual landscape effortlessly.
